# 📊 Mental Health Search Trends in Spain (2004–2025)

## 📌 Overview

This project analyzes Google search behavior in Spain to understand how interest in mental health topics has evolved over the past two decades.

Using Google Trends data, the analysis explores patterns in searches related to:
- Anxiety
- Depression
- Stress
- Panic attacks

The goal is not only to identify trends, but also to uncover deeper insights into how society engages with mental health topics online.

---

## 🎯 Objectives

- Analyze long-term trends in mental health-related searches
- Identify the impact of COVID-19 on search behavior
- Explore relationships between different mental health topics
- Understand whether the rise in online therapy reflects real demand or digital adoption

---

## 📂 Dataset

- Source: Google Trends (Spain)
- Period: 2004–2025
- Data type: Search interest over time (normalized index 0–100)

The dataset was built by combining multiple keyword trend files into a unified time series.

---

## ⚙️ Methodology

1. Data collection from Google Trends
2. Data cleaning and preprocessing
3. Time series analysis
4. Correlation analysis
5. Visualization of trends

---

## 📈 Key Insights

- Mental health-related searches have increased significantly over time
- A noticeable spike occurs during the COVID-19 pandemic
- Anxiety and depression show strong correlation patterns
- The growth in online therapy searches suggests both increased need and digital accessibility
